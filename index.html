Aspects of Car Culture that were Born or Made Popular in the JDM Era

  • Stance: adjusting the camber or angle of the wheels so that they slant inwards, usually mainly the front wheels.
  • Two steping: reving the engine over and over causing the some of the combustion explosions from the engine to escape out the exhaust pipe shooting flames and making loud popping sounds.
  • Drifting: puposefully losing traction in the back tires allowing the driving to slide or "drift" around corners or obstacles at an angle or nearly sideways.
  • Slamming cars: adjusting the suspension so that the car is lower to the ground and the wheels are tucked up inside the wheel wells a little bit.
  • Wide body: Putting on a kit or adjusting the body of the car to be wider, particularly around the wheel wells, to be able to fit wider tires and for asthetic.
